MAKMAKAN

Pinakbet

Pinakbet Ilocano is a Filipino version of cooking vegetables, fermented fish sauce (bagoong), and fried pork

Dinengdeng

Ilocos empanada is made of rice flour and stuffed with green papaya, longganisa sausages, and hard-boiled eggs.

Dinengdeng

It’s a tasty Filipino dish from the Ilocos region, known for its simple yet delicious blend of veggies and sometimes fish.

Dinakdakan

Dinakdakan is a traditional Ilocano dish often compared to Pampanga’s sisig, yet it stands out due to its distinct creamy texture.

Igado

Igado is a popular Ilokano dish that will make you want to keep eating more rice. The thick and rich texture of the dish add to the pleasure of indulging in this delicious meal.

Dinardaraan

Dinardaraan is a unique pork dish that is popular in Ilocos. It is made with pork, vinegar, and sugar, and is often referred to as "chocolate meat" due to its dark color.

Crispy Bagbagis

also known as crispy isaw or chicharon bituka, is made with deep fried pork intestines that are so crispy and crunchy in every bite. It's the perfect pair for an ice-cold beer!

Papaitan

Papaitan is a famous Ilocano soup dish mostly composed of cow or goat innards. The name of this dish was derived from the Filipino word “Pait”, which means “bitter”.

Hi-Bol

It is a paksiw miki noodle soup (with beef innards) or native beef tripe soup that originated from Ilocos. Almost like mami or batchoy but the taste is different than batchoy.
