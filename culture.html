CULTURE & TRADITIONS

1. Pottery-making (Damili)

Pottery-making (Damili) is central to San Nicolas' identity, earning it the title "Pottery Center of Ilocos Norte." Local artisans craft traditional clay pots (burnay), bricks, and other earthenware, a skill passed down through generations. The Damili Festival, celebrated annually, showcases this tradition through pottery-making competitions, exhibitions, and cultural performances.

2. Values and Lifestyle

The people of San Nicolas are known for their "panagtitinnulong" (community cooperation), hard work, and frugality, typical traits of Ilocanos. Respect for elders, close family ties, and pride in heritage are strongly upheld.

3. Festivals and Celebrations

Aside from festival, people also celebrates the Holy Week: Marked by solemn processions and reenactments of Christ's Passion, and Barangay fiestas: Each barangay celebrates its patron saint with food, music, and merriment.

4. Family and Community Gatherings

Celebrations like weddings, baptisms, and birthdays are occasions for extended families and neighbors to come together. Traditional Ilocano songs, dances, and games often enliven these gatherings.

5. Farming and Harvesting Rituals

Agriculture, particularly rice farming, is a traditional livelihood. Rituals such as panag-arado (plowing) and ani (harvest celebrations) involve prayers for blessings and good yield.

6. Livelihood Practices

Apart from pottery, locals engage in traditional weaving, fishing, and farming. Bargain markets and town fairs are also popular venues for showcasing local crafts and products.
